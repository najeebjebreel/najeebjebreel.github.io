Who I Am

About Me

Najeeb Jebreel is a senior postdoctoral researcher at the Universitat Rovira i Virgili (URV), specialising in machine learning (ML) privacy and security. He brings over a decade of diverse academic and professional experience in research, teaching, and technical leadership.

He currently serves in the CRISES Research Group at URV. He was also awarded the Juan de la Cierva 2024 Postdoctoral Fellowship by the Spanish Government (awarded 2025; to start 2026).

With a research portfolio of 20+ scientific publications, an h-index of 10, and over 548 citations, his work has appeared in high-impact journals such as IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, Neural Networks, Artificial Intelligence Review, and Knowledge-Based Systems.

His research excellence has been recognised through multiple prestigious awards, including the Best Paper Award at the CORE A-ranked PAKDD 2023 (among 869 submissions). He has presented at PAKDD (Japan 2023), IJCAI (CORE A★, South Korea 2024), and ESORICS (France 2025).

Beyond research, he contributes to high-profile projects funded by the European Commission, INCIBE, and the Agencia Española de Investigación. He also co-supervises Master's and PhD students, and collaborates with institutions such as Huawei Technologies and the Notre Dame–IBM Technology Ethics Lab.

He received his PhD from URV in 2023, graduating cum laude and earning the URV Extraordinary Doctorate Award. He is affiliated with the UNESCO Chair in Data Privacy at URV.

What I Work On

Research

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, privacy, and security — building AI systems that are robust, trustworthy, and aligned with human values. Three threads run through my research portfolio:

Federated Learning Security

Federated learning enables collaborative model training without sharing raw data, but introduces new attack surfaces. My work develops defences against label-flipping and Byzantine attacks, and proposes fragmented federated learning to simultaneously strengthen privacy and security guarantees in distributed settings.

Key papers: LFighter, Enhanced Security via Fragmented FL, FL-Defender, Byzantine Detection

Backdoor Attack Defence

Backdoor attacks embed hidden triggers in models that activate on specific inputs while remaining undetectable during normal evaluation. I introduced a layer-wise feature analysis framework that exposes these trojaned representations, earning the Best Paper Award at PAKDD 2023 and an invitation to present at IJCAI 2024 (CORE A★).

Key papers: DBALFA (PAKDD 2023), DBALFA Extended Abstract (IJCAI 2024), KeyNet

LLM Privacy & Unlearning

As large language models memorise vast amounts of training data, risks to privacy, copyright, and fairness intensify. My research advances machine unlearning — selectively erasing knowledge from trained models without full retraining — and rigorously analyses membership inference attacks to quantify and mitigate privacy exposure in modern LLMs.

Key papers: Digital Forgetting Survey, DP2Unlearning, Membership Inference Beyond Overfitting, Defenses against MIA

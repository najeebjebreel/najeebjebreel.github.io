About Me

I am an Ordinary Researcher at the Universitat Rovira i Virgili (URV), working within the CRISES Research Group. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, machine learning privacy, and security — with a focus on building systems that are robust, trustworthy, and ethically aligned.

With over a decade of diverse academic and professional experience, I have contributed to defensive mechanisms against backdoor and label-flipping attacks in federated learning, privacy-preserving ML, and the alignment and unlearning of large language models (LLMs).

My work has been recognised through multiple prestigious awards, including the Best Paper Award at the CORE A-ranked PAKDD 2023 (among 869 submissions). I have presented at top-tier venues such as IJCAI (CORE A★) and published in high-impact journals including Neural Networks and IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems.

I received my PhD in Computer Engineering and Mathematics of Security from URV in 2023, graduating cum laude and earning the URV Extraordinary Doctorate Award.

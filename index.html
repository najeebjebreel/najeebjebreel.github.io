About Me

I am an Ordinary Researcher at the Universitat Rovira i Virgili (URV), working within the CRISES Research Group. My research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence, machine learning privacy, and security. I specialize in developing secure and privacy-preserving machine learning systems that can withstand various forms of attacks.

With over a decade of diverse academic and professional experience, I have made significant contributions to the development of defensive mechanisms against backdoor attacks, enhancing federated learning privacy and security, and aligning large language models (LLMs) with ethical values.

My research excellence has been recognized through multiple prestigious awards, including the Best Paper Award at the CORE A-ranked PAKDD 2023 conference (among 869 submissions). I have also presented my work at top-tier international conferences such as IJCAI (CORE A* ranking) and published in high-impact journals like Neural Networks and IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems.

I received my PhD in Computer Engineering and Mathematics of Security from URV in 2023, graduating cum laude and earning the URV Extraordinary Doctorate Award. Prior to that, I completed my Master's in Computer Security Engineering and Artificial Intelligence at URV (2019) and my Bachelor's in Computer Science and Engineering from Hodeidah University, Yemen (2009).
